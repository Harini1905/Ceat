Vision, Mission & Objectives

To become a globally recognized leader in automation technologies, driving innovation and transforming industries through interdisciplinary research and cutting-edge solutions

Our mission is to foster interdisciplinary collaboration among academia, industry, and researchers to innovate and deliver sustainable automation solutions. We aim to empower industries through advanced technologies, enhance skill development, and contribute to societal progress by addressing real-world challenges

  • Foster world-class innovative research by publishing at least 20 high-impact papers and developing 5 smart automation solutions for manufacturing industries within the next five years to address societal and industrial challenges
  • Collaborate across disciplines, including mechanical engineering, computer science, and advanced sciences, to develop at least 10 integrated automation systems tailored for industries such as healthcare, energy, and manufacturing within the next three years.
  • Establish strategic partnerships with at least 15 industries, academic institutions, and research organizations within the next three years to facilitate knowledge exchange, execute 5 technology transfer projects, and complete 10 consultancy projects annually.
  • Nurture entrepreneurial ventures by incubating at least 10 start-ups annually and providing technical support to develop scalable automation solutions, with a target of 3 successful commercial deployments within five years

CeAT Subdivisions

Mechatronics & Automation

Mechatronics & Automation integrates mechanical engineering, electronics, and computer science to design intelligent systems that operate with minimal human intervention. This field is pivotal in developing advanced robotics, smart manufacturing systems, and autonomous technologies. For CeAT, this thrust area supports the creation of flexible industrial processes, assistive rehabilitation robots, and autonomous systems, driving innovation in automation aligning with Industry 4.0 principles.

3D Printing and Scanning

3D printing (additive manufacturing) and scanning revolutionize production by enabling rapid prototyping, customization, and cost-effective manufacturing. These technologies are widely used in aerospace, healthcare, automotive, and construction for creating complex, lightweight components. At CeAT, research in this area focuses on advancing materials, improving printing accuracy, and integrating hybrid manufacturing techniques which contributes to sustainable manufacturing practices and supports the development of customized solutions for various industries.

AIML and Image Processing

Artificial Intelligence (AI), Machine Learning (ML), and Image Processing empower systems to learn, analyze visual data, and make intelligent decisions. Applications include predictive maintenance, quality control, healthcare diagnostics, and autonomous navigation. CeAT leverages these technologies to develop intelligent manufacturing systems, optimize processes, and enhance automation in healthcare and surveillance.

Energy Automation

Energy automation focuses on optimizing energy generation, distribution, and consumption using advanced technologies like smart grids and renewable energy integration. This field addresses global challenges such as energy efficiency, sustainability, and reliable power supply. At CeAT, research in energy automation includes developing predictive models for energy usage, fault detection, and integrating renewable sources into existing grids.

Condition Monitoring (CM)

Machinery Condition Monitoring (MCM) uses sensors and data analytics to assess the health and performance of industrial machinery in real-time. MCM ensures operational safety, extends the lifespan of equipment, and reduces maintenance costs by detecting anomalies, predicting failures, and enabling proactive interventions. CeAT’s focus on MCM involves developing advanced sensor networks and AI-driven predictive models to monitor parameters such as vibration, temperature, and wear.
